<p align="justify">
I am an AI Researcher focused on adversarial robustness, trustworthy AI, and perception security for autonomous systems. I recently earned my Master of Science in Computer Engineering with a concentration in AI from <a href = "https://www.tntech.edu/"> Tennessee Technological University</a>, where I was advised by <a href ="https://sites.tntech.edu/shasan/"> Dr. Syed Rafay Hasan</a>.

My work spans adversarial machine learning, LiDAR and camera-based perception, edge AI deployment, and real-time defense mechanisms for safety-critical environments. Recently, I have investigated adversarial machine learning vulnerabilities in Ouster LIDAR and camera sensor fusion, engineering novel defenses optimized for edge devices like Nvidia Jetson.

I am actively interested in pursuing a PhD in robust machine learning, secure autonomy, and trustworthy multimodal perception.
</p>
<p align="justify"> Currently I work on <i> adversarial robustness </i> and <i> trustworthy AI </i>, focusing heavily on perception security for autonomous systems. I also work on using <i> adversarial machine learning </i> for LiDAR and camera-based perception to <i> deploy real-time defense mechanisms </i> in safety-critical edge AI environments. Furthermore, in my industry role, I build <i> GenAI-powered troubleshooting assistants </i> using Python and Azure OpenAI to automate diagnostics and improve incident response velocity. </p>

<p align="justify"> In the past, I have worked on advances in secure electronic health records by architecting <i> decentralized platforms </i> using Docker containerization and an IPFS cluster. Additionally, I have spent time developing <i> early-stage deep learning pipelines </i> for image classification, as well as engineering <i> scalable backend systems </i> and distributed ETL pipelines for high-traffic applications. </p>
